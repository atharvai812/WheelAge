Manifolds

In automotive engineering, a manifold is a component that allows multiple components to connect to each other. There are two main types of car manifolds: intake manifolds and exhaust manifolds.

Intake manifolds deliver the air-fuel mixture to the cylinders. They are typically made of aluminum or plastic, and they have a number of tubes or runners that connect to the individual cylinders.

Exhaust manifolds collect the exhaust gases from the cylinders and route them to the exhaust pipe. They are typically made of cast iron or stainless steel, and they have a number of pipes or tubes that connect to the individual cylinders.
