Fuel Pumps

The fuel pump is a critical component of a vehicle's fuel delivery system. It is responsible for pumping fuel from the fuel tank to the engine.

Function: The primary function of the fuel pump is to deliver a constant and pressurized supply of fuel to the engine. It ensures that the engine receives the required amount of fuel for combustion and power generation.

Types of Fuel Pumps: There are two main types of fuel pumps used in vehicles: mechanical fuel pumps and electric fuel pumps. Mechanical fuel pumps were more common in older vehicles and are driven by the engine's camshaft.
