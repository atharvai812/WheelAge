Radiator

An automobile radiator is a heat exchanger that is used to cool the engine of a car. It is typically located in the front of the car, and it is made up of a series of tubes and fins. The coolant, which is a mixture of water and antifreeze, flows through the tubes, and the air that passes through the fins helps to cool the coolant.

The radiator is an important part of the car's cooling system. If the radiator is not working properly, the engine can overheat, which can cause serious damage.

functions of an automobile radiator:

Cooling the engine

Maintaining the coolant temperature

Preventing the coolant from boiling
